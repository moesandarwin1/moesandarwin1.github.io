Early carrer life

Born in Madrid, Garnacho joined Atlético Madrid's youth system in 2015 from Getafe.

Carrer

Garnacho is eligible to play for his country of birth, Spain, and Argentina, as his mother is Argentine.[22] He made three appearances for the Spain under-18 team in 2021.On 7 March 2022, Garnacho was called up to the senior Argentina team as part of an initial 44-man squad for their two World Cup qualifiers that month.[24] He made it to the final 33-man squad for the fixtures,[25][26] but did not make an appearance in either game.[27] Garnacho made his debut for the Argentina under-20 side on 26 March 2022, when he started in a friendly match against the United States.[28] He scored four goals in four appearances[23] for the under-20 team at the 2022 Maurice Revello Tournament winning the Revelation Player and Goal of the Tournament awards.[29][30] In March 2023, he was called up once again to the senior Argentina team for two friendly matches against Panama and Curaçao,[31][32] but had to withdraw from the squad after suffering an ankle injury.[16] He made his debut for the senior team on 15 June 2023, during a friendly against Australia at Workers' Stadium, coming on as a substitute for Nicolás González during the second half of the game.[33]
